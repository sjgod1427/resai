How it works

Inject. Detect. Correct.

Three stages turn a biased recommendation feed into a measurably fairer one — without throwing away relevance.

1

Bias is injected, on purpose

A two-tower retrieval model scores ~13K real YouTube videos by similarity and engagement — systematically suppressing Educational, Documentary, Regional, and News content, just like real platforms do.

2

An LLM judges the feed

A structured LLM call (Groq Llama 3.3 70B) compares the biased feed against the user's actual preferences and history, flagging missing or over-represented genres.

3

The LLM rebuilds it, fairly

A second structured LLM call selects 30 videos from a pre-fetched candidate pool under hard fairness quotas — validated in Python, with a deterministic rule-based fallback if constraints aren't met — refined further through multi-round natural-language feedback.

Measured, not assumed

Five fairness metrics, every run

Every feed — biased, corrected, and ideal — is scored 0–100 on genre diversity, suppressed-category coverage, representation (Gini), language diversity, and overall fairness.
